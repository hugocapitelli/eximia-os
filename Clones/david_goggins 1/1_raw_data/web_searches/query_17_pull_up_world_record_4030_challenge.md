# David Goggins - Pull-Up World Record: 4,030 in 17 Hours

**Source:** Web Search - "David Goggins pull-up world record 4030 17 hours challenge"
**Date:** 2025-12-19

---

## The World Record

**Year:** 2013
**Total Pull-Ups:** 4,030
**Time:** 17 hours and 45 minutes
**Status:** Guinness World Record (at the time)

**Achievement:** After two failed attempts, David Goggins broke the Guinness World Record for pull-ups, going to 4,030 just to be sure.

---

## Training Leading Up to Record

### Six-Month Preparation

**Training Volume:**
- 40,000 pull-ups completed in 6 months prior to record attempt
- Systematic preparation and conditioning

### Total Pull-Ups (Training + Attempts)

**Combined Total:**
- Between training and three attempts: 67,000+ pull-ups in 9 months
- Demonstrates extreme dedication and physical toll

---

## The Failed Attempts

### First Attempt - The Today Show

**Location:** Live on The Today Show
**Pull-Ups Completed:** 2,500
**Result:** Failed

**Why It Failed:**
- Lactic acid buildup in muscles
- Had to pull out mid-attempt
- Public failure on national television

**Mental Impact:** Despite public embarrassment, decided to try again.

---

### Second Attempt

**Pull-Ups Completed:** 3,203
**Time:** 17.5 hours
**Result:** Failed

**Why It Failed:**
- Hands became too damaged to continue
- Severe blisters and tears
- Physical breakdown, not mental

**Lesson:** Sometimes body has actual limits, not just mental ones.

---

## The Successful Third Attempt

### The Final Push

**12-Hour Mark:**
- Already at 3,000 pull-ups
- Still needed 1,021 more for record
- Exhausted but determined

**17-Hour Mark:**
- Reached 4,021 (the record at the time)
- Continued to 4,030 "just to be sure"
- No doubt about breaking it

**Mental State:**
- Exhausted after 17 hours
- Two previous failures weighing on him
- Used Cookie Jar technique
- Accessed 40% rule to push through

---

## Physical Toll

### Hands

**Damage:**
- Severe blisters
- Torn skin
- Bleeding
- Second attempt stopped due to hand damage

**Recovery:**
- Had to heal between attempts
- Developed calluses
- Conditioned hands for third attempt

### Muscle Fatigue

**Challenges:**
- Lactic acid buildup
- Muscle failure
- Grip strength deterioration
- Full-body exhaustion

---

## The Philosophy Behind It

### Why Do This?

**Purpose:**
- Fundraising for Special Operations Warrior Foundation
- Demonstrate mental toughness
- Prove 40% rule in action
- Show what human body/mind can achieve

### The Cookie Jar in Action

**During Attempt:**
- Drew on past successes
- Remembered Navy SEAL training
- Visualized completing Badwater 135
- Used mental tools he preaches

---

## Impact & Legacy

### Proving the 40% Rule

**Demonstration:**
- At 3,000 pull-ups, mind screaming to quit
- Only 60% done according to 40% rule
- Pushed through to 4,030
- Physical proof of philosophy

### Inspiration

**For Others:**
- Showed extreme limits of human endurance
- Demonstrated power of not quitting
- Three attempts = persistence pays off

**Key Lesson:**

## Current Record Status

**Note:** The record has since been broken by others:
- Truett Hanes: 4,100 pull-ups in 17.5 hours
- Corstian Marel: 4,040 pull-ups in 13 hours 46 minutes (2023)
- Records continue to be pushed

**Goggins' Place:** Pioneered extreme pull-up challenges, inspiring others to attempt.
