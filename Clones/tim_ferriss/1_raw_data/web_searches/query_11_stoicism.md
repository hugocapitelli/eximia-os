## Core Stoic Practices (Daily Integration)

### 1. Fear-Setting (Premeditatio Malorum)

**Inspired by:** Seneca's premeditation of adversity

**Process:**
- Visualize worst-case scenarios
- Define fears explicitly
- Mitigate emotional impact
- Overcome paralysis

**Ferriss Quote:**
> "Defining one's fears is as crucial as defining one's goals."

**Impact:**
- Helped achieve significant successes
- Avoided major disasters
- Regular practice (monthly/quarterly)

### 2. Daily Reading of Stoic Texts

**Routine:**
- 10-15 minutes/day reading Stoic letters
- Primarily Seneca

**"The Tao of Seneca":**
- Turned Seneca's letters into audiobook series
- Listens regularly
- Makes Stoicism accessible

**Morning Practice:**
- Reads Marcus Aurelius's "Meditations" in morning
- Prepares for unexpected challenges
- Sets philosophical tone for day

### 3. Practicing Discomfort / Voluntary Hardship

**Seneca's "Practicing Poverty":**

**Applications:**
- Intermittent fasting (several days/month)
- Cold exposure (cold showers)
- Dressing plainly on purpose
- Training to be ashamed only of truly shameful things
- Not superficial judgments

**Purpose:**
- "Toughen the soul" for greater stress
- Fortify against misfortune
- Build resilience through choice

### 4. Focus on Controllables

**Core Teaching:**
- Distinguish what IS vs ISN'T within control
- Conserve limited resources
- Deploy energy optimally

**Application:**
- Don't respond to online attacks (uncontrollable)
- Focus on work quality (controllable)
- Internal responses over external judgments

### 5. Resilience & Detachment from Criticism

**Managing "Catastrophe of Success":**
- Success brings criticism
- Stoicism provides buffer

**Ferriss Mantra:**
> "Never as bad as they say, never as good as they say."

**Result:**
- Maintains level head
- Doesn't internalize external judgments
