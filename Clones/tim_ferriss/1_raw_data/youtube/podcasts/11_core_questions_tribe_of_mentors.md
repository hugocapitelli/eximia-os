# Tim Ferriss - The 11 Core Interview Questions (Tribe of Mentors)

**Source:** "Tribe of Mentors" book + podcast usage  
**Asked to:** 130+ world-class leaders  
**Purpose:** Distill wisdom from top performers  
**Date Collected:** 2026-01-19

---

## The 11 Questions

### 1. Books as Gifts
**Question:**
> "What is the book (or books) you've given most as a gift, and why? Or what are one to three books that have greatly influenced your life?"

**Purpose:**
- Reveals values
- Shows what guest considers essential knowledge
- Demonstrates influence chain

**Clone Application:** Use to understand guest's intellectual framework

---

### 2. Best Purchase Under $100
**Question:**
> "What purchase of $100 or less has most positively impacted your life in the last six months (or in recent memory)?"

**Purpose:**
- Small tactics with big impact
- Actionable for listeners
- Shows attention to details
- 80/20 principle in action

**Clone Application:** Identifies high-leverage, accessible tools

---

### 3. Favorite Failure
**Question:**
> "How has a failure, or apparent failure, set you up for later success? Do you have a 'favorite failure' of yours?"

**Purpose:**
- Reframes failure as learning
- Shows resilience
- Demonstrates growth mindset
- **Stoic undertone:** Amor fati (love of fate)

**Clone Application:** Essential for understanding relationship with setbacks

---

### 4. Billboard Message
**Question:**
> "If you could have a gigantic billboard anywhere with anything on it — metaphorically speaking, getting a message out to millions or billions — what would it say and why?"

**Purpose:**
- Core message/philosophy
- What guest thinks world needs most
- Condensed wisdom

**Clone Application:** Captures essential philosophy in soundbite

---

### 5. Best Investment
**Question:**
> "What is one of the best or most worthwhile investments you've ever made? (Could be an investment of money, time, energy, etc.)"

**Purpose:**
- Broad definition of "investment"
- Shows priorities (money vs. time vs. relationships)
- ROI mindset

**Clone Application:** Understanding value system

---

### 6. Unusual Hab it or Absurd Thing You Love
**Question:**
> "What is an unusual habit or an absurd thing that you love?"

**Purpose:**
- Humanizes guest
- Shows personality
- Often reveals productivity hacks
- **Playful** - builds rapport

**Clone Application:** Adds character, relatability

---

### 7. New Belief/Behavior/Habit (Last 5 Years)
**Question:**
> "In the last five years, what new belief, behavior, or habit has most improved your life?"

**Purpose:**
- Recent evolution
- Still testing/refining
- Practical and current
- Shows growth mindset

**Clone Application:** Captures latest thinking, not just fixed beliefs

---

### 8. Advice to College Student
**Question:**
> "What advice would you give to a smart, driven college student about to enter the 'real world'? What advice should they ignore?"

**Purpose:**
- Contrarian thinking (what to IGNORE)
- Practical career/life guidance
- Often challenges conventional wisdom
- Two-part: Do + Don't

**Clone Application:** Reveals what guest sees as BS vs. valuable

---

### 9. Bad Recommendations in Your Field
**Question:**
> "What are bad recommendations you hear in your profession or area of expertise?"

**Purpose:**
- Contrarian filter
- Industry myths debunked
- Critical thinking
- Authority to challenge norms

**Clone Application:** Understanding nuance in domain expertise

---

### 10. Saying No / Focus
**Question:**
> "In the last five years, what have you become better at saying no to (distractions, invitations, etc.)? What new realizations and/or approaches helped? Any other tips?"

**Purpose:**
- Elimination (DEAL framework tie-in)
- Focus and priorities
- Subtraction as skill
- **Essentialism**

**Clone Application:** Core to Tim's philosophy - less but better

---

### 11. Overwhelm Management
**Question:**
> "When you feel overwhelmed or unfocused, or have lost your  focus temporarily, what do you do? (If helpful: What questions do you ask yourself?)"

**Purpose:**
- Mental health / resilience
- Tactical recovery methods
- Self-awareness
- **Everyone faces this** - shows humanity

**Clone Application:** Captures coping mechanisms, self-talk

## Question Design Philosophy

### Common Themes:

**1. Specific over General**
- Not "what's your advice" but "what advice to college students"
- Constraints force better answers

**2. Time-Bounded**
- "Last 5 years" / "Last 6 months"
- Prevents generic, rehearsed answers
- Forces reflection on recent evolution

**3. Contrarian Elements**
- "What advice to IGNORE"
- "Bad recommendations"
- Challenges conventional wisdom

**4. Action-Oriented**
- "What do you do" not "what do you think"
- Tactical over theoretical
- Implementable insights

**5. Personal & Vulnerable**
- Favorite failure
- Feeling overwhelmed
- Unusual habits
- Builds trust through vulnerability

---

## Tim's Interview Sequencing

### Typical Flow:

**Easy → Hard**
- Starts with books (comfortable topic)
- Moves to failures (more vulnerable)
- Ends with overwhelm (very personal)

**Warm-Up Value:**
- First questions get guest talking
- Build rapport
- Establish safety
- Then dive deeper

## Additional Signature Questions

### Beyond the 11:

**Priority Question:**
> "If I could only work two hours per week on my business, what would I do?"
- **Purpose:** 80/20 principle application
- Forces identification of vital few

**Contrarian Belief:**
> "Tell me something that's true that almost nobody agrees with you on."
- **Purpose:** Original thinking
- Tests for independent thought

**Simplification:**
> "What would this look like if it were easy?"
- **Purpose:** Overcome over-complication
- **Tim's personal mantra**

**Morning Routine:**
- Consistent across many episodes
- "Win the morning, win the day"
- Reveals priorities through action

## Conversational Voice in Questions

### Tim's Phrasing Patterns:

**Framing:**
- "If helpful..." → Gives guest permission to interpret
- "Or..." → Offers alternative angle
- "(Could be...)" → Expands definition

**Specificity:**
- "$100 or less" - exact constraint
- "Last six months" - specific timeframe
- Not vague "what's important to you?"

**Curiosity Markers:**
- "Walk me through..."
- "How specifically..."
- "Can you elaborate..."
