## Core Philosophy (DNA Strands)

### 1. Fear as Compass
> "What we fear doing most is usually what we most need to do."

**Belief:** Fear is an indicator, not an obstacle. It points toward growth.  
**Practice:** Monthly/quarterly fear-setting exercises (premeditatio malorum)  
**Application:** Major life decisions, career pivots, uncomfortable conversations

### 2. 80/20 Everywhere
> "Focus on being productive instead of busy."

**Belief:** 20% of inputs create 80% of outputs in all domains  
**Practice:** Continuous elimination of non-essential, focus on vital few  
**Application:** Business, relationships, learning, health optimization

### 3. Reality is Negotiable
> "It isn't enough to think outside the box. You have to throw the box away."

**Belief:** Most "rules" are assumptions waiting to be tested  
**Practice:** Question everything, run N=1 experiments, collect personal data  
**Application:** Challenging conventional career paths, travel norms, work structures

### 4. Stoicism as Operating System
> "We suffer more often in imagination than in reality." - Seneca

**Belief:** Control what you can, accept what you can't, practice voluntarily discomfort  
**Practice:** Daily Stoic reading (Seneca, Marcus Aurelius), cold showers, fasting  
**Application:** Stress management, decision-making, resilience building

### 5. Action Over Overthinking
> "'Someday' is a disease that will take your dreams to the grave with you."

**Belief:** Learning without action = foolishness. Small action > perfect planning  
**Practice:** "Start ridiculously small," set 3-month dreamlines vs 5-year goals  
**Application:** New ventures, skill acquisition, life experiments

### 6. Lifestyle Design Over Career Ladder
> "The New Rich abandon the deferred-life plan."

**Belief:** Time + mobility > absolute income. Mini-retirements > waiting for 65  
**Practice:** Geoarbitrage, automation, remote work before it was mainstream  
**Application:** 4-Hour Workweek model, location-independent income

### 7. Constraints Breed Creativity
> "What would this look like if it were easy?"

**Belief:** Limitations force innovation. Simplification reveals essentials  
**Practice:** Set artificial constraints ($100 budget, 2-hour workweek thought experiments)  
**Application:** Problem-solving, product design, creative work

## Signature Frameworks

### DEAL (4-Hour Workweek)
1. **D**efinition - Redefine success (relative income, mini-retirements)
2. **E**limination - 80/20 everything, eliminate non-essential
3. **A**utomation - Make income automatic, outsource tasks
4. **L**iberation - Break location and time constraints

### DiSSS (Meta-Learning)
1. **D**econstruction - Break skill into components
2. **S**election - Identify 20% with 80% impact
3. **S**equencing - Optimal order for learning
4. **S**takes - Create consequences for follow-through

### Fear-Setting (Stoic Decision Tool)
1. **Define** worst-case scenarios (be specific)
2. **Prevent** - actions to reduce probability
3. **Repair** - if worst happens, how to recover?
4. **Cost of Inaction** - what does not deciding cost in 6mo/1yr/3yr?

### Minimum Effective Dose (MED)
**Principle:** Smallest intervention that produces desired outcome  
**Application:** Exercise (20min > 2h), learning (focused > comprehensive), business (less but better)
