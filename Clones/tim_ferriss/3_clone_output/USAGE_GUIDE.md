## How to Get Best Results

### 1. Be Specific

**Instead of:** "I want to be more productive."  
**Try:** "I spend 3h/day on email but feel like I'm not getting important work done. How would Tim approach this?"

**Why:** Clone can apply specific frameworks (in this case: Batch Processing, 80/20 Analysis, Pareto's Principle)

### 2. Provide Context

**Instead of:** "Should I quit my job?"  
**Try:** "I'm 28, have $20K saved, hate my corporate job but fear instability. Want to start a business but don't know if timing is right."

**Why:** Clone can run Fear-Setting exercise tailored to your situation

### 3. Ask About Frameworks

**Good Questions:**
- "How does the DEAL framework apply to X?"
- "Can you walk me through Fear-Setting for Y decision?"
- "What woulds DiSSS look like for learning Z?"
- "How would Tim apply 80/20 to my situation?"

**Why:** Leverages clone's deep knowledge of Tim's systematic approaches

### 4. Test and Iterate

**After getting advice:**
- Implement smallest version (N=1 experiment)
- Track results
- Return with data: "I tried X, here's what happened, now what?"

**Why:** Tim's philosophy is experimentation and personal data over theory

## Example Conversation Flow

### User Opening
"I want to write a book but keep procrastinating."

### Clone Response
"Let's diagnose this. A few questions:

1. Is it actually procrastination, or are you avoiding because it's not truly exciting?  
   (Tim's filter: 'Hell Yeah or No' - if not excited, wrong project)

2. What specifically stops you when you sit down to write?  
   (Fear of bad writing? Unclear outline? Too ambitious?)

3. How much time per week could you realistically commit?

Based on your answers, I can share:
- **If it's exciting but scary:** Fear-Setting exercise
- **If it's overwhelming:** 'Start ridiculously small' (Tim's '2 crappy pages/day')
- **If it's unclear:** DiSSS to deconstruct book writing
- **If it's time:** Batch processing + 80/20 on current commitments

What resonates most?"

### User: "It's overwhelming. Don't know where to start."

### Clone:
"Perfect - this is where Tim's 'Start Ridiculously Small' approach shines.

**Here's the system:**
1. Quota: TWO CRAPPY PAGES per day. Not good pages. Crappy ones.  
   (Permission to suck removes perfectionism paralysis)

2. Write BEFORE you manage/admin. 'Make before manage.'  
   (Creative work in morning, admin after)

3. Don't edit while writing. Separate creation from editing.  
   (Jerry Seinfeld principle: 95% of work is editing, but do it AFTER drafting)

4. Seinfeld's constraint: 'You don't have to write, but you can't do anything else.'  
   (Dedicated 30-60 min, no distractions, boredom eventually → writing)

Try this for 2 weeks. Track: How many days did you hit 2 crappy pages?

Often the problem isn't motivation - it's the bar being too high. Lower it. Build momentum.

Want to run Fear-Setting on 'What if I write a bad book?' or dive straight into starting?"

→ Ask: "Can you elaborate on X?" or "Walk me through Y step-by-step"
