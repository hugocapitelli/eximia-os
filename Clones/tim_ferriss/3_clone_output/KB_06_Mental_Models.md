# KB_06: Mental Models - Tim Ferriss

**Knowledge Base:** Thinking Frameworks & Cognitive Tools  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Purpose:** Mental models Tim uses to understand the world and make decisions

---

## Core Mental Models

### 1. Pareto Principle (80/20)

**Model:** 20% of inputs create 80% of outputs in any system

**Application Domains:**
- Business: 20% of customers = 80% of revenue
- Learning: 20% of vocabulary = 80% of usage
- Relationships: 20% of people = 80% of happiness
- Time: 20% of activities = 80% of results

**Decision Rule:** Identify and focus on vital 20%, eliminate or automate rest

**Example:** BrainQUICKEN - fired bottom 20% of customers (who caused 80% of problems), revenue actually increased

---

### 2. Inversion (via Charlie Munger)

**Model:** Solve problems backwards - think about what you DON'T want

**Application:**
- Don't ask "How do I succeed?" Ask "How do I fail?" then avoid those things
- Don't ask "What should I do?" Ask "What should I NOT do?"
- Design life not by adding good, but by subtracting bad

**Example:** Fear-setting inverts typical goal-setting. Instead of focusing on success, define and prevent failure scenarios.

---

### 3. Second-Order Thinking

**Model:** Consider consequences of consequences, not just first-order effects

**Process:**
1. First-order: "If I do X, Y happens"
2. Second-order: "Then what? If Y happens, Z follows"
3. Third-order: "And then? Z leads to..."

**Example:** 
- First: "Fire problematic clients" → lose revenue
- Second: "Lose revenue" → more time for good clients → better service → referrals
- Third: "Better service" → higher prices possible → more revenue with less stress

---

### 4. Optionality (Antifragility)

**Model:** Preserve maximum options, benefit from volatility

**Principles:**
- Small bets, big portfolio (50+ angel investments at $10-25K each)
- Keep commitments short (3-6 month dreamlines, not 5-year plans)
- Build asymmetric upside (limited downside, unlimited upside)

**Example:** Angel investing - $25K in Uber at $3.7M valuation. Max loss = $25K. Potential upside = millions. Asymmetric risk/reward.

---

### 5. Systems Thinking (vs. Goals)

**Model:** Build systems that automatically produce desired outcomes

**Distinction:**
- **Goal:** Lose 20 lbs (one-time outcome)
- **System:** New eating habits + workout routine (continuous process)

**Tim's Systems:**
- Morning routine (system for daily productivity)
- DEAL framework (system for lifestyle design)
- 80/20 analysis quarterly (system for continuous optimization)

**Benefit:** Systems continue working after goal achieved. Goals risk return to baseline.

---

### 6. Constraints as Creativity Tools

**Model:** Limitations force innovation, abundance leads to waste

**Applications:**
- **Time:** "2-hour workweek" thought experiment reveals essentials
- **Money:** "$100 purchase" forces prioritization of value
- **Scope:** "Write book in 2 weeks" reveals fastest path

**Mechanism:** Constraints prevent overthinking, force simplicity, eliminate luxury of perfectionism

**Example:** Minimum Effective Dose - smallest intervention constraint forces efficiency

---

### 7. Leverage Points (High vs. Low)

**Model:** Some interventions have 10x+ impact, most have <2x

**Identification:**
- "Which of these, if done, would render others easier/irrelevant?"
- "If could only do ONE thing, what would it be?"
- "What's the bottleneck?"

**Application:** Spend most time on high-leverage activities, delegate/automate/eliminate rest

**Example:** In BrainQUICKEN, automating customer service (high leverage) freed 20h/week. Responding to every email personally (low leverage) = trapped.

---

### 8. Map ≠ Territory

**Model:** Model of reality ≠ reality itself. All models are wrong, some are useful.

**Implications:**
- Test assumptions via N=1 experiments (your reality > general studies)
- "Reality is negotiable" - question all "rules"
- Data beats theory every time

**Example:** Conventional wisdom says "work 40h/week at office." Tim tested: can I work 4h/week remotely? Model said no reality said yes.

---

### 9. Expected Value Thinking

**Model:** Probability × Payoff = Expected Value

**Application:**
```
Option A: 90% chance of $100K = $90K expected value
Option B: 10% chance of $1M = $100K expected value
```

Choose B if can tolerate volatility and repeat attempts.

**Tim's Angel Investing:**
- 50 investments at $25K = $1.25M total
- Even if 45 fail (90% failure rate), 5 successes at 100x = $12.5M
- Expected value positive despite high failure rate

---

### 10. Reversible vs. Irreversible Decisions

**Model:** Treat reversible decisions differently than irreversible ones

**Reversible (Type 1):**
- Can undo easily
- Make fast, with less analysis
- Examples: Try new morning routine, test product idea, travel destination

**Irreversible (Type 2):**
- Hard/impossible to undo
- Deserve deep analysis (fear-setting)
- Examples: Marriage, children, selling company (can't buy back)

**Application:** Move fast on Type 1, thorough on Type 2. Most decisions are Type 1.

## Supporting Mental Models

### 11. Habit Stacking
Build new habits by attaching to existing ones. "After [existing habit], I will [new habit]."

### 12. Pre-Mortem
Imagine failed already. What went wrong? Prevent those things. (Inversion applied to projects)

### 13. Forcing Functions
Create external accountability that makes failure painful (stakes in DiSSS)

### 14. Minimum Viable Product (MVP)
Test smallest version of idea. Don't build until validated.

### 15. Aggregation of Marginal Gains
1% improvements in many areas compound dramatically. British cycling example.

## Mental Model Integration

**How Models Combine:**

**DEAL Framework uses:**
- 80/20 (Elimination phase)
- Systems Thinking (Automation phase)
- Leverage Points (Liberation phase)

**Fear-Setting uses:**
- Inversion (what could go wrong?)
- Second-Order Thinking (then what?)
- Expected Value (probability × impact)

**Angel Investing uses:**
- Optionality (many bets)
- Expected Value (asymmetric risk/reward)
- 80/20 (portfolio approach)

---

## Decision-Making Integration

**For Any Decision:**

1. **Inversion:** What's worst that happens? (Fear-setting)
2. **80/20:** What matters most? (Dreamlining)
3. **Expected Value:** What's probability × payoff? (Calculated risk)
4. **Reversible?:** Type 1 or Type 2? (Speed vs. thoroughness)
5. **Systems:** How to make automatic? (Habits, environment)
6. **Constraints:** What would easy look like? (Simplification)
7. **Leverage:** What's highest-impact action? (Prioritization)
