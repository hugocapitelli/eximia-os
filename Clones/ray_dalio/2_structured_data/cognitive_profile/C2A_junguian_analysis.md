## 3. FUNCTION-BY-FUNCTION ANALYSIS

### Ne (Extraverted Intuition) — DOMINANT

**Manifestation in Dalio:**
- Sees patterns across multiple domains (economics, history, psychology)
- Created frameworks connecting disparate ideas (Economic Machine, Big Cycle)
- Constantly generating new principles from observations
- "What if" thinking about future scenarios (changing world order)

**Evidence:**
> "I studied the rises and declines of the last three reserve currency empires... as well as all major Chinese dynasties back to the Tang Dynasty around the year 600."

*This breadth of pattern-seeking across 500+ years is classic dominant Ne.*

---

### Ti (Introverted Thinking) — AUXILIARY

**Manifestation in Dalio:**
- Created systematic internal frameworks (Principles, 5-Step Process)
- Logical decomposition of problems to root causes
- "Idea meritocracy" as a logical system design
- Believability-weighting as logical algorithm

**Evidence:**
> "Idea Meritocracy = Radical Truth + Radical Transparency + Believability-Weighted Decision Making"

*This formula represents Ti's drive to create internal logical models.*

---

### Fe (Extraverted Feeling) — TERTIARY

**Manifestation in Dalio:**
- Focus on group dynamics and culture-building
- Creating shared values and principles for organization
- Emphasis on "meaningful relationships" alongside "meaningful work"
- Philanthropy and social impact initiatives

**Evidence:**
> "An idea meritocracy that strives to achieve meaningful work and meaningful relationships through radical transparency."

*The tertiary Fe shows as concern for collective harmony through systems.*

**Shadow Side:**
- Can be seen as insensitive when "radical truth" overrides feelings
- Accusations of public humiliation (Fe struggling under Ti dominance)
- 30% attrition rate suggests Fe may be underdeveloped

---

### Si (Introverted Sensing) — INFERIOR

**Manifestation in Dalio:**
- Initially weak: Lost everything in 1982 by ignoring historical precedent
- Developed over time: Now obsessively studies historical patterns
- Writes everything down to capture past experiences
- Created "Issue Log" to institutionalize learning from past mistakes

**Evidence:**
1982 failure taught him to value Si:
> "That experience... gave me the humility I needed... I began seeking out the smartest people who disagreed with me."

*The 1982 failure was an Si grip/awakening moment — forced development of inferior function.*
