## 1. META-HEURISTICS (Governing Principles)

### M001: Radical Truth
**Trigger:** Any decision or interaction
**Action:** Seek accurate understanding of reality above all else
**Quote:** "Truth — more precisely, an accurate understanding of reality — is the essential foundation for producing good outcomes."

### M002: Radical Transparency
**Trigger:** Any organizational process
**Action:** Make information visible to all who can benefit
**Quote:** "The more people can see what is happening—the good, the bad, and the ugly—the more effective they are at deciding the appropriate ways of handling things."

### M003: Idea Meritocracy
**Trigger:** Group decision-making
**Action:** Let the best idea win regardless of source
**Formula:** `Idea Meritocracy = Radical Truth + Radical Transparency + Believability-Weighted Decision Making`

## 6. THE FIVE-STEP PROCESS (Detailed)

### Step 1: Have Clear Goals
- Know what you want
- Don't confuse goals with desires
- Recognize that having almost everything means being able to have almost anything
- Maturity = rejecting good alternatives for better ones

### Step 2: Identify and Don't Tolerate Problems
- View problems as potential improvements screaming at you
- Don't avoid confronting problems because of harsh realities
- Be precise in specifying problems
- Don't confuse causes of problems with real problems

### Step 3: Diagnose Problems to Get at Root Causes
- Focus on "what is" before deciding "what to do"
- Distinguish proximate causes from root causes
- Recognize that knowing what someone is like helps predict what to expect from them

### Step 4: Design a Plan
- Go back before going forward (understand where you've been)
- Think of your problem as a set of outcomes from a machine
- Remember there are many paths to achieving your goals
- Think of your plan as a movie script

### Step 5: Push Through to Completion
- Great planners who don't execute go nowhere
- Good work habits are underrated
- Establish clear metrics to ensure you're following your plan

## 7. DECISION-MAKING ALGORITHMS

### Believability-Weighted Decision Algorithm
```
1. Gather opinions from all relevant parties
2. For each person, assess:
   - Track record in this domain
   - Ability to explain reasoning
3. Assign believability weight (0-100)
4. Calculate weighted average of positions
5. Compare equal-weighted vs believability-weighted
6. If aligned → decision resolved
7. If not aligned → deeper investigation or RP override
```

### Pain Processing Algorithm
```
1. Experience pain/failure
2. DO NOT: fight, flee, or freeze
3. DO: Pause and breathe
4. Reflect: What happened?
5. Diagnose: What's the root cause?
6. Extract: What's the principle/lesson?
7. Document: Write it down
8. Integrate: Add to decision framework
9. Progress: Apply learning going forward
```

## 8. SIGNATURE MENTAL MODELS

### The Machine Model
- View any system (economy, organization, self) as a machine
- Identify inputs, processes, outputs
- Debug by examining machine components
- Improve by upgrading machine parts

### The Two Yous Model
- Higher-level self (prefrontal cortex): Rational, long-term, principled
- Lower-level self (amygdala): Emotional, reactive, defensive
- Success = getting higher self to manage lower self

### The Cycle Model
- Everything moves in cycles
- Short-term debt cycle: 5-8 years
- Long-term debt cycle: 75-100 years
- Empire cycle: Rise → Peak → Decline
- Personal cycle: Goal → Failure → Learning → New Goal
