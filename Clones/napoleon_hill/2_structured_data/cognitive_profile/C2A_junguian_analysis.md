## Function Analysis

### Fe (Extraverted Feeling) — DOMINANT

**Evidence:**
- Entire life dedicated to helping others succeed
- Created frameworks for mass consumption
- Public speaking career spanning decades
- Emphasis on "Master Mind" (group harmony)
- Built movements around shared values

**Quotes Demonstrating Fe:**
> "It is literally true that you can succeed best and quickest by helping others to succeed."

> "Think twice before you speak, because your words and influence will plant the seed of either success or failure in the mind of another."

**Behavioral Manifestations:**
- Positioned self as teacher/guide
- Focused on inspiring masses
- Created accessible, shareable principles
- Used emotional appeals in writing

### Ni (Introverted Intuition) — AUXILIARY

**Evidence:**
- Long-term vision (20-year research project)
- Pattern recognition across successful people
- Abstract principle extraction
- Future-oriented philosophy ("Your future is created by what you do today")

**Quotes Demonstrating Ni:**
> "Cherish your visions and your dreams as they are the children of your soul, the blueprints of your ultimate achievements."

> "Hold a picture of yourself long and steadily enough in your mind's eye, and you will be drawn toward it."

**Behavioral Manifestations:**
- Synthesized patterns from 500+ interviews
- Created universal principles from specifics
- Focused on "what could be" not "what is"
- Visualization techniques central to philosophy

### Se (Extraverted Sensing) — TERTIARY

**Evidence:**
- Action-oriented advice ("Begin at once")
- Physical metaphors (fire/heat, carbon/steel)
- Practical, concrete steps
- Present-moment awareness in later teachings

**Quotes Demonstrating Se:**
> "Don't wait. The time will never be just right."

> "Action is the real measure of intelligence."

**Behavioral Manifestations:**
- Emphasis on immediate action
- Use of vivid, sensory language
- Adapted to circumstances (multiple career pivots)
- Physical energy in public speaking

### Ti (Introverted Thinking) — INFERIOR

**Evidence:**
- Logical inconsistencies in claims
- Fraud charges suggest poor internal logic checks
- Philosophy relies more on faith than rigorous proof
- "Believe it and achieve it" over analytical verification

**Weak Points:**
- Made unverifiable claims (Carnegie meeting)
- Multiple business failures due to poor planning
- Relied on emotional persuasion over logical proof
- Philosophical system has logical gaps
