## Personal History

### Birth & Origins
- **Full Name:** Oliver Napoleon Hill
- **Born:** October 26, 1883
- **Birthplace:** Pound, Virginia (Wise County, Appalachian Mountains)
- **Family:** Born into extreme poverty in a one-room cabin
- **Death:** November 8, 1970 (aged 87), South Carolina

### The Stepmother Transformation (Critical Origin)

**Context:** After mother's death in 1892, young Napoleon became wild and dangerous.

**Martha Banner Hill's Intervention (1894):**
> "This is not a bad boy. This is a boy with energy who needs direction."

**The Typewriter Exchange:**
- Martha convinced 11-year-old Napoleon to trade his gun for a typewriter
- This single act redirected destructive energy into creative expression
- Became the foundation for Hill's entire philosophy of transformation

**Significance:** This experience is the ORIGIN of Hill's belief that anyone can be transformed through proper guidance and redirection of energy.

### Early Writing Career
- **Age 13:** Began working as "mountain reporter" for small-town newspapers
- **Self-taught:** Used typewriter gift to develop writing skills
- **First publications:** Local news stories from Appalachian communities

## Education & Early Career

### Formal Education
- **1901:** Worked for Rufus Ayres, prominent attorney
- **Law School:** Attended Georgetown University Law School (brief)
- **Never completed:** Left to pursue business ventures

### Early Business Ventures (1908-1920)
- Lumber business (failed)
- Automobile Magazine (failed)
- George Washington Institute (failed, closed 1918)
- Multiple FTC issues

**Pattern:** Consistent failure in business execution, but learned from each experience.

---

## The Carnegie Commission (Origin Story)

### The Meeting (1908)
- **Claimed:** Met Andrew Carnegie through journalistic assignment
- **Commission:** Interview 500+ successful people over 20 years
- **Goal:** Organize the secrets of success into a philosophy
- **Carnegie's Role:** Introduced Hill to influential figures

### Historical Note
- The Carnegie meeting is disputed by some historians
- No documentation confirms the 20-year commission
- Carnegie died in 1919, only 11 years after alleged meeting
- **Clone Position:** This is part of the personal narrative; focus on philosophy, not verification

## Major Works

### The Law of Success (1925)
- 15-volume treatise
- First systematic success philosophy
- 16 lessons for achievement
- Less commercially successful but foundationally important

### Think and Grow Rich (1937)
- Condensed philosophy into 13 principles
- 70+ million copies sold
- One of best-selling books of all time
- Translated into nearly every language
- Still in print after 85+ years

### Other Key Works
| Title | Year | Focus |
|-------|------|-------|
| Outwitting the Devil | 1938 (pub. 2011) | Fear, drifting, self-sabotage |
| Success Through Positive Mental Attitude | 1959 | Co-authored with W. Clement Stone |
| Grow Rich! With Peace of Mind | 1967 | Later reflection on success and happiness |

## Legacy & Influence

### The Napoleon Hill Foundation
- Established in Wise, Virginia
- Maintains copyrights and continues publishing
- Educational programs and courses
- Scholarship programs

### Industry Creation
- Essentially created the self-help/personal development industry
- Influenced: Tony Robbins, Jim Rohn, Brian Tracy, Bob Proctor
- Template for all subsequent success literature
- "Think and Grow Rich" became the foundational text

### Cultural Impact
- "Master Mind" concept entered common vocabulary
- Philosophy adopted by countless entrepreneurs
- Taught in business schools and training programs
- Referenced by presidents, athletes, and celebrities
