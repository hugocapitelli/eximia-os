## Major Works

### The Law of Success (1925)

- **Original Title:** The Law of Success in Sixteen Lessons
- **Format:** Series of brochures/lessons
- **Description:** "The Master Wealth-Builder's Complete and Original Lesson Plan for Achieving Your Dreams"
- **Significance:** First major work to codify Hill's philosophy
- **Note:** First work to publicly thank Andrew Carnegie

### Think and Grow Rich (1937)

- **Published:** 1937 (during the Great Depression)
- **Sales:** 20 million copies by Hill's death (1970)
- **Current Sales:** Over 100 million copies globally
- **Status:** Among the 10 best-selling self-help books of all time
- **Significance:** Distilled the 16 lessons into 13 principles
- **Context:** Offered hope during the Great Depression

### Outwitting the Devil (1938/2011)

- **Written:** 1938
- **Published:** June 2011 (73 years later!)
- **Format:** Dialogue with "The Devil"
- **Why Delayed:** Wife Annie Lou didn't want it published due to religious concerns
- **Path to Publication:**
  1. Hill died 1970 - manuscript to Annie Lou
  2. Annie Lou died 1984 - to Dr. Charles Johnson (Napoleon Hill Foundation)
  3. Eventually to Don Green (CEO of Foundation)
  4. Sharon Lechter asked to edit
  5. Released 2011 with annotations
- **Content:** Explains why people fail even after following Think and Grow Rich

## The Napoleon Hill Foundation

- **Founded By:** Napoleon Hill before his death
- **Type:** Nonprofit educational institution
- **Mission:** Perpetuate Hill's philosophy of leadership, self-motivation, and individual achievement
- **Activities:**
  - Publishing posthumous works
  - Maintaining archives
  - Educational programs
  - Licensing intellectual property

## Book Themes by Era

### Early Works (1925-1937)
- Focus on business success
- More direct, tactical advice
- Less mystical language

### Middle Works (1937-1960)
- Peak of fame
- More refined philosophy
- Integration of spiritual concepts
- "Infinite Intelligence" terminology

### Late Works (1960-1970)
- More introspective
- Peace of mind emphasis
- Legacy concerns
- Collaboration with others (W. Clement Stone)
