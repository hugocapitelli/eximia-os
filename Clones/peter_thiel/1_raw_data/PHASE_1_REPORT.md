## Research Findings Summary

### Biography Highlights

- Born October 11, 1967 in Frankfurt, Germany
- Emigrated to US at age 1
- Childhood in South Africa/Namibia (apartheid era)
- Stanford BA Philosophy (1989), Stanford Law (1992)
- Chess master, German fluency maintained
- Founded The Stanford Review (conservative publication, 1987)
- Co-founded PayPal (1998), CEO after Musk ousted
- PayPal sold to eBay for $1.5B (2002) - Thiel earned $55M
- First outside investor in Facebook (2004) - $500k → $1B+
- Co-founded Palantir (2003) - CIA-backed data analytics
- Founded Founders Fund (2005) - contrarian VC
- Thiel Fellowship launched (2011) - $200k to skip college
- Funded Gawker lawsuit (2016) - $10M → Gawker bankruptcy
- Trump supporter/RNC speaker (2016)
- Mentored J.D. Vance → donated $15M to Senate campaign
- Net worth: ~$27.5B (2025)

### Philosophy Core

1. **Mimetic Theory (Girard)** - Human desire is imitative
2. **Monopoly > Competition** - Competition destroys profits
3. **Zero to One** - Create new things, don't copy
4. **Definite Optimism** - Plan specifically for better future
5. **Secrets Exist** - Important truths hiding in plain sight
6. **Power Law** - Few winners take almost everything
7. **Last Mover Advantage** - Better to dominate late than first

### Communication Patterns Identified

- Deliberate, measured pace
- Heavy philosophical references
- Inverts conventional wisdom
- "I think..." framing
- Comfortable with ambiguity
- Tolkien naming (Palantir, Mithril)
- Dry, sardonic humor
