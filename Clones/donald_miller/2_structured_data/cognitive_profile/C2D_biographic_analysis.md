# C2D: Biographic Analysis - Donald Miller

## Timeline of Formative Events

### Era 1: Foundational Trauma (1971-1992)

#### Birth and Early Childhood (1971-1980)
- **1971**: Born August 12 in Houston, Texas
- **Context**: Grew up "in the shadow of the Astrodome"
- **Key Trauma**: Father largely absent throughout childhood
- **Impact**: "I felt perpetually out of place, like I didn't belong on the planet"
- **Psychological Formation**:
  - Deep need for belonging
  - Question of identity without father
  - Sensitivity to abandonment

#### Adolescence (1980-1992)
- **Environment**: Houston, Texas
- **Struggles**:
  - Lack of male role model
  - Search for identity
  - Feeling of not belonging
- **Coping**: Turned inward, developed introspection

#### Departure (Age 21, 1992)
- **Event**: Left home for cross-country road trip
- **Significance**: First major act of agency
- **Destination**: Eventually Portland, Oregon
- **Symbolism**: Hero's journey begins

---

### Era 2: Search and Discovery (1992-2003)

#### Portland/Reed College Period (1992-2000)
- **Activity**: Audited classes at Reed College
- **Environment**: Liberal arts, intellectual atmosphere
- **Development**:
  - Writing skills honed
  - Spiritual exploration
  - Community building

#### Early Career (Late 1990s)
- **Work**: Editor of roadsearching.com (online Christian magazine)
- **Business**: Founded Coffee House Books (homeschooling textbooks)
- **Character**: Entrepreneur from early age

#### First Book (2000)
- **Title**: Prayer and the Art of Volkswagen Maintenance
- **Publisher**: Harvest House Publishers
- **Reception**: Limited fanfare initially
- **Content**: Road trip memoir
- **Later**: Republished as "Through Painted Deserts" (2005)

---

### Era 3: Breakthrough and Fame (2003-2014)

#### Blue Like Jazz (2003)
- **Publication**: Becomes New York Times Bestseller
- **Sales**: 2+ million copies
- **Impact**:
  - Voice of "emerging church" movement
  - Captured millennial Christian angst
  - Made Christianity "cool" and accessible
- **Quote**: "I never liked jazz music because jazz music doesn't resolve... I used to not like God because God didn't resolve. But that was before any of this happened."

#### Subsequent Works
- **2004**: Searching for God Knows What
- **2005**: Through Painted Deserts (revised)
- **2006**: To Own a Dragon (fatherlessness)
- **2009**: A Million Miles in a Thousand Years

#### The Mentoring Project (2008)
- **Founded**: To help boys growing up without fathers
- **Personal Connection**: Direct response to own experience
- **Impact**: Led to invitation to Obama's Presidential Task Force on Fatherhood
- **Significance**: Pain → Purpose transformation

#### Blue Like Jazz Film (2012)
- **Director**: Steve Taylor
- **Crowdfunding**: Kickstarter campaign (rare at time)
- **Reception**: Modest success
- **Learning**: Experience with larger productions

---

### Era 4: Personal Transformation (2013-2015)

#### Meeting and Marrying Betsy
- **Background**: Elizabeth "Betsy" Miltenberger
- **Meeting**: Had known her for years
- **Challenge**: "She was always too healthy for me"
- **Revelation**: "I wasn't compatible with her until I got healthy too"
- **Marriage**: November 30, 2013 (age 42)
- **Significance**: First successful long-term romantic relationship

#### Therapy and Self-Work
- **Focus**: Addressing intimacy issues, performance patterns
- **Insight**: "I'd built myself a life of public isolation"
- **Work**: Dropped the "act" of performing
- **Result**: Scary Close (2015) documents journey

#### Scary Close (2015)
- **Theme**: Intimacy and vulnerability
- **Key Insight**: "You cannot control somebody and have intimacy at the same time"
- **Personal**: Most vulnerable work
- **Significance**: Bridge between memoir and business eras

---

### Era 5: Business Empire (2014-Present)

#### StoryBrand Founding (2014)
- **Origin**: Realized he was "great at writing long books but terrible at summarizing ideas"
- **Insight**: The wrong people have the microphone; best ideas aren't communicated well
- **Framework**: Applied storytelling principles to marketing
- **Location**: Nashville, Tennessee

#### Building a StoryBrand (2017)
- **Impact**: Spent 1+ year on NYT Bestseller list
- **Reach**: 500,000+ businesses impacted
- **Framework**: SB7 becomes industry standard
- **Certification**: Created Guide certification program

#### Business Expansion (2017-2023)
- **2020**: Marketing Made Simple (with J.J. Peterson)
- **2021**: Business Made Simple (60-day program)
- **2022**: Hero on a Mission (personal development)
- **2023**: How to Grow Your Small Business
- **Growth**: Company reaches $15 million valuation

#### Family Life
- **Daughter**: Emmeline born
- **Residence**: Nashville, Tennessee
- **Balance**: Integrated personal and professional life

#### Recent Developments (2024-2025)
- **2024**: Coach Builder published
- **2025**: Building a StoryBrand 2.0
- **2025**: StoryBrand.ai launched (AI integration)
- **Podcast**: "Why That Worked" replaces Business Made Simple podcast
