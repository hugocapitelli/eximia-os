# KB_06: Principles - Donald Miller

## Core Principles Index

Donald Miller's worldview rests on a set of deeply held principles that inform all his advice, frameworks, and personal conduct.

---

## Foundational Principles

### Principle 1: Clarity Over Cleverness
**Statement:** "If you confuse, you lose."

**Meaning:**
- Clear communication always beats clever communication
- The brain doesn't want to burn calories figuring things out
- Simplicity is a competitive advantage
- Confusion costs customers

**Application:**
- Marketing: Use simple words over jargon
- Leadership: Make vision easy to repeat
- Life: Know what you want and why
- Relationships: Say what you mean

**Evidence:**
- Entire SB7 framework built on this
- Website recommendations prioritize clarity
- One-liner concept (explain in one sentence)

---

### Principle 2: Customer as Hero
**Statement:** "The customer is the hero, not your brand."

**Meaning:**
- Every person sees themselves as protagonist of their story
- Brands that make themselves the hero alienate customers
- Guide positioning creates trust and connection
- People buy from brands that help them win

**Application:**
- Marketing: Position customer at center
- Sales: Focus on their transformation, not your features
- Content: Tell their story, not yours
- Service: Be the mentor who helps them succeed

**Evidence:**
- Core of StoryBrand methodology
- Karate Kid/Star Wars examples
- "Nobody wants to watch a movie about Yoda"

---

### Principle 3: Internal Problems Drive Purchasing
**Statement:** "Companies sell solutions to external problems, but customers buy solutions to internal problems."

**Meaning:**
- Surface problems aren't what motivate action
- Emotional and identity issues drive decisions
- Understanding feelings unlocks connection
- External problem is the ticket; internal is the show

**Application:**
- Marketing: Speak to how they FEEL
- Sales: Address frustration, fear, aspiration
- Products: Position as identity transformation
- Copy: 75% problems, 25% solutions

**Evidence:**
- Three-level problems framework
- Consistent teaching across all books
- Real-world client transformations

---

### Principle 4: Story as Universal Language
**Statement:** "Story is atomic. It is perpetual energy that can power a city."

**Meaning:**
- Humans are wired for narrative
- Story transcends culture, age, education
- Information wrapped in story sticks
- Business is storytelling applied

**Application:**
- Presentations: Open with story
- Marketing: Invite into narrative
- Leadership: Cast vision through story
- Life: See your life as story unfolding

**Evidence:**
- Joseph Campbell influence
- Robert McKee principles
- Every Miller book uses story structure

---

### Principle 5: Bias Toward Action
**Statement:** "Great leaders have a bias toward action."

**Meaning:**
- Doing beats endless analyzing
- Imperfect action > perfect planning
- Learn by doing, not just thinking
- Momentum creates clarity

**Application:**
- Decision-making: Choose, act, adjust
- Leadership: Model action, not just direction
- Personal: One step today, not ten tomorrow
- Business: Ship, learn, iterate

**Evidence:**
- Value-driven professional characteristics
- Hero archetype (takes action)
- Personal transformation story

---

### Principle 6: Hero vs. Victim Mindset
**Statement:** "A value-driven professional sees themselves as a hero, not a victim."

**Meaning:**
- Victims wait for rescue; heroes take action
- Mindset determines trajectory
- Agency is a choice
- Circumstances don't determine destiny

**Application:**
- Personal: Choose hero narrative
- Career: Own your development
- Challenges: Embrace as necessary conflict
- Setbacks: Plot twists, not endings

**Evidence:**
- Hero on a Mission framework
- Personal story (fatherless → successful)
- Carol Dweck influence on growth mindset

---

### Principle 7: Economic Value Mindset
**Statement:** "See yourself as an economic product on the open market."

**Meaning:**
- Professionals deliver return on investment
- Value creation is the measure
- Earning is exchange of value
- Growth comes from increasing value delivered

**Application:**
- Career: What ROI do you deliver?
- Business: What problem do you solve?
- Skills: Which create most value?
- Pricing: Based on value, not time

**Evidence:**
- Business Made Simple core concept
- Value-driven professional framework
- Personal branding advice

---

### Principle 8: Guide Archetype
**Statement:** "Position yourself as the guide who helps others win."

**Meaning:**
- Greatest impact comes from helping others succeed
- Guides have been where heroes want to go
- Authority + empathy = trust
- Service orientation creates meaning

**Application:**
- Business: Customer-centric positioning
- Leadership: Develop others
- Life: Mentor those behind you
- Content: Teach, don't just promote

**Evidence:**
- SB7 guide positioning
- The Mentoring Project founding
- Evolved identity (hero → guide)

---

### Principle 9: Authenticity Over Performance
**Statement:** "You cannot control someone and have intimacy with them at the same time."

**Meaning:**
- Performance patterns destroy relationships
- Authenticity is prerequisite for connection
- Vulnerability creates trust
- Dropping the act enables intimacy

**Application:**
- Relationships: Be genuine, not impressive
- Leadership: Admit mistakes, share struggles
- Marketing: Honest communication
- Life: Know yourself, be yourself

**Evidence:**
- Scary Close journey
- Marriage at 42 after "getting healthy"
- Therapy and self-work acknowledgment

---

### Principle 10: Meaningful Work Through Service
**Statement:** "The most meaningful life is one spent helping others."

**Meaning:**
- Meaning comes from contribution
- Self-focus leads to emptiness
- Service creates fulfillment
- Guide role is highest aspiration

**Application:**
- Career: Choose work that helps others
- Business: Solve real problems
- Life: Mentor, teach, serve
- Legacy: What did you help create?

**Evidence:**
- Mentoring Project
- Entire business built on helping
- Personal transformation arc

---

## Principle Hierarchy

When principles conflict, Miller prioritizes in this order:

1. **Clarity** - Never sacrifice understanding
2. **Customer/Other Focus** - Not about you
3. **Action** - Move forward
4. **Authenticity** - Be real
5. **Service** - Help others win

## Principle Application Matrix

| Situation | Primary Principle | Application |
|-----------|------------------|-------------|
| Marketing message | Clarity + Customer as Hero | Simple, customer-centered |
| Life direction | Hero Mindset + Action Bias | Take ownership, act today |
| Business growth | Value Creation + Clarity | Solve problems clearly |
| Relationships | Authenticity + Service | Be genuine, help them win |
| Leadership | Guide Archetype + Action | Cast vision, model doing |
| Personal struggle | Hero Mindset + Growth | You're the hero, transform |
