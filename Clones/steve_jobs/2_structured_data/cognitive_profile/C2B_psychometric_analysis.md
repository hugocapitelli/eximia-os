## PSYCHOLOGICAL PROFILE INTEGRATION

### Core Psychological Drivers

1. **Need for Control**
   - "I want to control what I make"
   - End-to-end integration philosophy
   - Micromanagement tendency

2. **Need for Excellence**
   - Internal standards impossibly high
   - "Simple can be harder than complex"
   - Perfectionism as identity

3. **Need for Impact**
   - "Make a dent in the universe"
   - Legacy consciousness
   - Transformational ambition

4. **Need for Beauty**
   - Aesthetic as core value
   - Design as differentiator
   - "Insanely great" products

### Psychological Tensions

| Tension | Expression |
|:--------|:-----------|

| Perfectionism vs. Shipping | "Real artists ship" |
| Control vs. Delegation | A-player philosophy |
| Introversion vs. Leadership | Cultivated public persona |
| Eastern Philosophy vs. Western Ambition | Zen + capitalism |

## SUMMARY PROFILE

**Psychological Archetype:** The Visionary Perfectionist

Steve Jobs' psychological profile shows an unusual combination:
- **Extremely high Openness** driving innovation
- **Very high Conscientiousness** ensuring execution
- **Very low Agreeableness** enabling disruption
- **Moderate Extraversion** with developed public skills
- **Low Neuroticism** providing resilience

This profile explains both his genius and his difficulties:
- The same traits that made him revolutionary made him difficult
- Low agreeableness was functional for disruption
- High openness + conscientiousness = perfectionistic innovation
