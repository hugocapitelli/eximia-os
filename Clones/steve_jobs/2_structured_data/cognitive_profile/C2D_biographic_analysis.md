# C2D BIOGRAPHIC ANALYSIS: Steve Jobs

## BIOGRAPHICAL TIMELINE

### PHASE 1: ORIGINS (1955-1971)

| Date | Event | Impact |
|:-----|:------|:-------|
| Feb 24, 1955 | Born in San Francisco | Adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs |
| 1955 | Given up for adoption | Core wound: abandonment, drive to prove worth |
| 1957 | Sister Patricia adopted | Family complete |
| 1959 | Family moves to Mountain View | Heart of Silicon Valley |
| 1960s | Works with father in garage | Learns craftsmanship, attention to detail |
| 1968 | Enters Homestead High School | Meets Bill Fernandez |
| 1969 | Fernandez introduces Wozniak | Beginning of Apple partnership |
| 1970 | First computer experience | HP Explorer Club |

**Formative Influences:**
- Paul Jobs: Craftsmanship ethic, garage tinkering
- Clara Jobs: Early reading, unconditional support
- Adoption: "I was abandoned. I needed to prove myself."

---

### PHASE 2: AWAKENING (1972-1976)

| Date | Event | Impact |
|:-----|:------|:-------|
| Sep 1972 | Enrolls at Reed College | Parents' life savings |
| Dec 1972 | Drops out of Reed | "Couldn't see the value" |
| 1973 | Audits calligraphy class | Future Mac typography |
| 1973 | Works at Atari | $5/hour, Nolan Bushnell mentorship |
| 1974 | Trip to India (7 months) | Spiritual awakening, Eastern philosophy |
| 1974 | Returns, embraces Zen Buddhism | Meets Kobun Chino Otogawa |
| 1975 | Attends Homebrew Computer Club | Personal computing vision begins |
| 1976 | Wozniak shows Apple I | "Let's sell it" |

**Transformative Moment: India Trip**
- Sought enlightenment at Maharaj Ji's ashram (he had died)
- 7 months wandering, experimenting with Eastern philosophy
- Quote: "Western rational thought is not an innate human characteristic"
- Integration: Zen + Technology = Apple aesthetic

### PHASE 3: APPLE ASCENT (1976-1985)

| Date | Event | Impact |
|:-----|:------|:-------|
| Apr 1, 1976 | Apple Computer founded | Jobs (26%), Wozniak (26%), Wayne (10%) |
| 1976 | Apple I sold at $666.66 | First revenue, ~200 units |
| 1977 | Apple II launched | Color graphics revolution |
| 1977 | Mike Markkula invests $250K | Adult supervision arrives |
| 1978 | Sales: $7.8M | Beginning of scale |
| 1978 | Lisa Brennan born | Denied paternity (shameful period) |
| 1979 | Visits Xerox PARC | Sees graphical interface, mouse |
| 1980 | Apple IPO: $100M+ | Jobs worth $200M+ at 25 |
| 1980 | Sales: $117M | Explosive growth |
| 1981 | IBM PC launches | Competition intensifies |
| 1983 | Lisa computer fails | $10,000 too expensive |
| 1983 | Recruits John Sculley from Pepsi | "Do you want to sell sugar water?" |
| 1984 | Macintosh launches | "1984" Super Bowl ad |
| 1984 | Mac struggles after initial sales | $2,495 too high, limited software |
| 1985 | Loses power struggle to Sculley | Board sides against Jobs |
| Sep 1985 | Resigns from Apple | Sells all but 1 share |

**Transformative Moment: Firing from Apple**
- Quote: "Getting fired was the best thing that could have ever happened to me"
- Quote: "The heaviness of being successful was replaced by the lightness of being a beginner"
- Allowed creative rebirth at NeXT and Pixar

### PHASE 4: WILDERNESS YEARS (1985-1996)

| Date | Event | Impact |
|:-----|:------|:-------|
| Sep 1985 | Founds NeXT Computer | $12M initial investment |
| Feb 1986 | Buys Pixar from Lucasfilm | $5M + $5M capital |
| 1988 | NeXT Cube launched | Too expensive, sales disappoint |
| 1989 | NeXTSTEP OS released | Revolutionary but niche |
| 1991 | Pixar signs Disney deal | 3 animated films contract |
| 1991 | Marries Laurene Powell | Buddhist ceremony by Kobun |
| 1991 | Son Reed born | First of three children with Laurene |
| 1993 | NeXT exits hardware | Software-only company |
| 1994 | Considered selling Pixar | Nearly sold to Microsoft, Hallmark |
| 1995 | Toy Story releases | $373M worldwide, IPO success |
| 1995 | Gives "Lost Interview" | Candid assessment of Apple's decline |
| 1996 | Apple acquires NeXT: $427M | Jobs returns as "advisor" |

**Lessons Learned:**
- NeXT: "Sometimes when you innovate, you make mistakes"
- Pixar: Patience, trusting creative teams
- Leadership maturity, less micromanagement
- Quote: "I'm pretty sure none of this would have happened if I hadn't been fired from Apple"

## TRANSFORMATIVE MOMENTS ANALYSIS

### The Five Pivots

1. **Adoption (1955)**
   - Created drive to prove worth
   - Quote: "I wanted to meet my biological mother to thank her for not aborting me"

2. **India Trip (1974)**
   - Shifted worldview from purely Western to East-West integration
   - Established lifelong Zen practice
   - Informed design philosophy

3. **Xerox PARC Visit (1979)**
   - Saw the future of computing
   - Quote: "Within ten minutes it was obvious this was how all computers would work"

4. **Firing from Apple (1985)**
   - Forced maturation
   - Quote: "The heaviness of being successful was replaced by the lightness of being a beginner"

5. **Cancer Diagnosis (2003)**
   - Reinforced mortality perspective
   - Stanford speech focus on death as clarity tool
