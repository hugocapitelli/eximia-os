### Full Summary:

**Nvidia has hired Alison Wagonfeld**, an executive who previously worked at Google, to serve as its first Chief Marketing Officer (CMO). This new role will centralize marketing functions that were previously managed by different executives. According to a Wall Street Journal source familiar with the matter, all marketing and communication teams within Nvidia will report directly to Wagonfeld starting in February 2026.

Wagonfeld's LinkedIn post confirms her transition from Google Cloud, where she played a pivotal role over nearly ten years, building it into a $60 billion business. She leaves Google at the end of January 2026 and joins Nvidia as its new CMO. In her statement, Wagonfeld highlights the strong partnership between Google and Nvidia while expressing enthusiasm for leading marketing efforts in another AI leader.

Nvidia's rapid growth is attributed to its dominant position in providing GPUs used for advanced AI applications. This surge accelerated after the launch of ChatGPT by OpenAI in 2022. As a result, the company has seen significant financial success, reporting record sales of $57 billion (R$307 billion) in the most recent quarter, marking a 62% increase from the previous year.

In anticipation of future market demands, Nvidia is expanding its focus beyond chip training to include improving inference capabilities—executing trained AI models in real-world applications. This strategic shift prompted an agreement with Groq, a startup specializing in inference chips, worth $20 billion.

This new leadership appointment underscores Nvidia's commitment to staying ahead in the rapidly evolving AI landscape and preparing for the next phase of technological advancements.
