## Assessment Tools & Rubrics

### Process Assessment

**Collaboration Rubric Dimensions:**
- Participation (quantity and quality)
- Listening (building on others' ideas)
- Contribution (bringing resources, ideas)
- Responsibility (completing assigned tasks)

**Self-Directed Learning Rubric:**
- Learning issue formulation (specific, researchable)
- Resource quality (credible, appropriate)
- Depth of understanding (can explain, not just repeat)
- Connection to problem (applies findings)

---

### Product Assessment

**Solution Quality Rubric:**
- Completeness (addresses all aspects of problem)
- Evidence-based (uses research to justify)
- Feasibility (realistic constraints)
- Communication (clear presentation)

**Knowledge Application Rubric:**
- Accuracy (correct information)
- Transfer (applies to new context)
- Integration (connects multiple concepts)
- Justification (explains reasoning)

---

### Peer Assessment Tools

**Peer Evaluation Form:**
```
Rate each team member (1-5) on:
1. Contributed ideas and resources
2. Listened respectfully to others
3. Completed assigned tasks on time
4. Helped the team stay focused

Provide specific example of this person's contribution:
[Text field]

One thing this person could improve:
[Text field]
```

**Self-Assessment Prompts:**
- My most valuable contribution was...
- I struggled with...
- Next time I would...
- I learned that...

## Implementation Checklists

### Starting PBL: First-Time Checklist

**Before First PBL:**
- [ ] Read foundational text (Barrows or Hmelo-Silver)
- [ ] Observe experienced PBL facilitator (if possible)
- [ ] Design problem aligned with learning objectives
- [ ] Pilot problem with colleague (feedback)
- [ ] Prepare scaffolding resources
- [ ] Create assessment rubrics
- [ ] Brief students on PBL expectations

**During First PBL:**
- [ ] Orient students to PBL process
- [ ] Model whiteboard use
- [ ] Set collaboration norms with students
- [ ] Facilitate first session (don't lecture!)
- [ ] Checkpoint SDL progress
- [ ] Gather formative feedback

**After First PBL:**
- [ ] Debrief with students (what worked/didn't)
- [ ] Self-reflect on facilitation
- [ ] Revise problem for next iteration
- [ ] Share experience with colleagues

---

### Scaling PBL: Department/School Checklist

**Planning Phase:**
- [ ] Secure admin support (time, resources)
- [ ] Form PBL implementation team
- [ ] Conduct needs assessment
- [ ] Set realistic timeline (3-5 years full adoption)

**Professional Development:**
- [ ] Facilitate PBL workshop for faculty
- [ ] Arrange PBL observations (visit PBL school)
- [ ] Establish PLC (Professional Learning Community)
- [ ] Ongoing coaching/mentoring

**Curriculum Design:**
- [ ] Map curriculum for PBL opportunities
- [ ] Develop problem library
- [ ] Align assessments with PBL goals
- [ ] Create shared resources (rubrics, templates)

**Institutional Support:**
- [ ] Adjust class schedules (longer blocks if needed)
- [ ] Allocate SDL time/space (library, study halls)
- [ ] Communicate with parents (what PBL is, why)
- [ ] Budget for resources, training

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge 1: "I don't have time to cover content AND do PBL"

**Solutions:**
- **Hybrid model:** Some PBL, some lecture (80/20 or 60/40)
- **Curricular prioritization:** What's essential vs. nice-to-know?
- **Trust the process:** PBL often covers MORE when students self-direct
- **Long-term view:** Retention from PBL reduces review time

---

### Challenge 2: "Students complain they're not being taught"

**Solutions:**
- **Explain PBL rationale:** Share research on effectiveness
- **Gradual introduction:** Start small (one PBL), increase over time
- **Visible facilitation:** Students may not recognize teaching when it's questioning
- **Patience:** Initial discomfort is normal, improves after 2-3 PBLs

---

### Challenge 3: "Administration/parents don't support PBL"

**Solutions:**
- **Invite observation:** Show (don't tell) PBL in action
- **Share student work:** Showcase depth of learning
- **Present research:** Meta-analyses, effect sizes
- **Start voluntary:** Pilot with willing teachers, expand based on success

---

### Challenge 4: "I'm uncomfortable not being the expert"

**Solutions:**
- **Reframe role:** You're coaching experts-in-training
- **It's okay to not know:** Model SDL when you don't know answer
- **Focus on process:** Teach HOW to learn, not just WHAT to learn
- **Trust students:** They'll surprise you with their capability

---

### Challenge 5: "Grading group work is unfair"

**Solutions:**
- **Individual accountability:** Peer assessment, individual quizzes
- **Process grades:** Assess SDL work, reflection (individual)
- **Multiple data points:** Don't rely solely on group product
- **Transparent rubrics:** Students know expectations upfront

## Quick Start Guide

### Week 1: Learn
- Read this KB and KB_01 (Foundations)
- Listen to "The Project" podcast episode
- Review 2-3 sample problems from PBLWorks

### Week 2: Design
- Identify learning objectives for upcoming unit
- Draft problem scenario (use REAL framework)
- Create whiteboard template
- Design assessment rubric

### Week 3: Pilot
- Run problem with small group or colleague
- Get feedback on clarity, difficulty, engagement
- Revise problem

### Week 4: Implement
- Orient students to PBL (20 minutes)
- Facilitate first PBL session
- Support SDL phase
- Facilitate application session
- Reflect with students

### Week 5: Reflect & Iterate
- What worked? What didn't?
- Student feedback?
- Revise for next time
- Share experience with peer

## Facilitator Professional Development

### Competencies for PBL Facilitators

**Core Skills:**
1. **Socratic questioning** - Ask, don't tell
2. **Strategic silence** - Wait time for thinking
3. **Scaffolding judgment** - When to help, when to let struggle
4. **Group dynamics** - Reading and managing team interactions
5. **Time management** - Balancing structure and flexibility

### Training Pathways

**Self-Directed:**
- Read foundational texts
- Watch PBL facilitator videos
- Practice with peer group

**Formal Training:**
- PBLWorks courses (online, in-person)
- University certificates (e.g., McMaster, Maastricht)
- Institutional workshops

**Experiential:**
- Co-facilitate with experienced PBL teacher
- Join PBL community of practice
- Iterate: Each PBL improves facilitation
